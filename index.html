Renewed Motivation

Posted: May 17, 2022

I recently completed the foundations section of The Odin Project. The final project was to build a calculator that would do basic arithmetic (add, subtract, divide, multiply). I was expecting the project to take well over a week to complete but I ended up finishing it in only four days. I felt so proud of myself for completing it so quickly and it made me realize just how much I've learned in just over a month.

After completing this project, I truly feel like I can do anything if I put my mind to it. My confidence is at an all-time high. I went through a period about a week ago where I felt like maybe I wasn't cut out for this industry, but now I feel much closer to actually doing this full-time.

I still have a lot to learn. There is still a long road ahead, but I am incredibly excited for what my future holds.

Adding to the Site

Posted: May 7, 2022

Today I decided to take a break from studying to work on my own projects. This is good practice and will help solidify what I've been learning. At this point, I'm nearly done with the foundations section of The Odin Project. Before I move on to the final project, I wanted to read through the entire JavaScript portion to make sure I really have a firm grasp of what I've learned so far. I'm taking the weekend off to give my brain a bit of a refresh.

I'm still really enjoying learning about web development, but I'd be lying if I said that my motivation wasn't starting to wane a little. I can't help but feel a little overwhelmed by all that I've been learning and I sometimes worry that I'm not progressing as quickly as I would like. But, I was warned that this would happen. One of the first sections of TOP was all about staying motivated. It, as well as several online articles on the subject, said that everyone who learns to code goes through the same journey. At first your motivation is really high, but as you progress and realise how difficult it can actually be, you start to doubt if you're really cut out for it. The people who are currently employed as software engineers or web developers are the ones who persevered and made it through that dip.

If I ever feel like I'm not progressing at the rate that I would like to be, I have to remind myself of how far I have come so far. A month ago, I knew nothing about web development. In that short time, I've managed to learn enough to build this website. Imagine what I'll know one month from now.

My First Blog Post

Posted: April 23, 2022

Welcome to my first website! I recently started teaching myself web development through The Odin Project. I started about a month ago and am progressing nicely. I'm starting this website as a journal of my progress that I can look back on in the future to see how far I've come.

So far I've learned about HTML, which is used to build the basic structure of the website (adding text, pictures, etc.) and CSS, which is used to style the website (changing colors, changing fonts, and placing the text where you want it). In the last two days I started learning about JavaScript which is used to make webpages interactive.
